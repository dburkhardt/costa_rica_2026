Sport Fishing Charter

Tamarindo is one of Costa Rica's premier sport fishing ports. April is peak season for mahi-mahi, roosterfish, and yellowfin tuna, with sailfish and marlin also in the mix. Half-day and full-day charters depart daily from the Tamarindo marina.

The experience

What to expect

Charter boats range from 28-foot center consoles (inshore fishing for roosterfish) to 40+ foot sport fishers (offshore for mahi-mahi, tuna, and billfish). Most half-day trips head 15–30 miles offshore where the continental shelf drops off and the big fish congregate.

April is one of the best months for fishing in Guanacaste. The water is warm and calm, baitfish are abundant, and mahi-mahi (dorado) are everywhere. Roosterfish — Costa Rica's signature inshore game fish — are also very active along the rocky shoreline. Catch-and-release is standard for billfish; mahi-mahi and tuna can be kept, and many restaurants will cook your catch for dinner.

  • Inshore (half-day): Roosterfish, snapper, jacks along the coast
  • Offshore (half/full-day): Mahi-mahi, yellowfin tuna, sailfish, marlin
  • All gear, bait, tackle, and captain/mate included
  • Boats typically hold 4–6 anglers comfortably
  • No experience needed — the captain and mate handle everything
  • Catch-and-release for billfish; keep mahi-mahi and tuna
  • Some operators include lunch, drinks, and fish cleaning
  • Many restaurants will cook your catch (Pangas Beach Club is a favorite)

Practical info

How to book & what to bring

  • Booking: JW Marriott concierge, or directly through Tamarindo Sportfishing, Capullo Sportfishing, or Kingpin Sportfishing
  • What to bring: Sunscreen, hat, sunglasses, camera, light long-sleeve shirt, seasickness meds if prone
  • What's included: Boat, captain, mate, all gear/tackle/bait, fishing license, drinks and snacks (varies)
  • Getting there: 15-minute drive to Tamarindo marina. Most operators offer hotel pickup.
  • Tip: Half-day trips (5 hrs) are plenty for most people. Full-day trips go further offshore for bigger game.
  • Cook your catch: Pangas Beach Club and Dragonfly Bar & Grill in Tamarindo will prepare your fresh catch for dinner
