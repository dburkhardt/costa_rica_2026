Rincón de la Vieja Day Trip

An active volcano national park with bubbling mud pots, natural hot springs, jungle waterfalls, and volcanic steam vents — all just 1.5 hours from the JW Marriott. This is a wilder, less-touristy alternative to Arenal with genuine volcanic activity you can see and feel.

The experience

What to expect

Rincón de la Vieja is one of Costa Rica's most active volcanoes, and the national park surrounding it offers a different experience from the beach resorts. The landscape is raw and primordial — volcanic mud pots bubble and hiss, fumaroles shoot steam into the air, and hot rivers flow through dense tropical forest.

Most day trips combine the national park with a nearby adventure lodge (Buena Vista, Hacienda Guachipelin, or Vandara) that offers hot springs pools, volcanic mud baths, zip-lining, tubing, horseback riding, and waterfall hikes. You can do as much or as little as you want — some people come just for the hot springs and mud bath, others do the full adventure combo.

  • Volcanic mud pots and fumaroles — active geothermal features you can walk right up to
  • Natural hot springs pools — mineral-rich volcanic water in jungle settings
  • Volcanic mud bath — cover yourself in warm mineral mud, let it dry, rinse off
  • Waterfalls: La Cangreja (blue waterfall), Oropéndola falls
  • Zip-lining through the forest canopy
  • River tubing through volcanic canyons
  • Horseback riding to volcanic steam vents
  • Jungle hiking trails through primary forest
  • Wildlife: howler monkeys, coatis, agoutis, toucans, parrots

Practical info

How to book & what to bring

  • Booking: JW Marriott concierge, Buena Vista Lodge, Hacienda Guachipelin, Vandara Hot Springs, or Tour Guanacaste
  • What to bring: Hiking shoes, swimsuit (for hot springs/mud bath), towel, sunscreen, water (lots!), hat, camera, change of clothes
  • What's included: Varies by package — basic includes park entry + guide; combo packages add hot springs, mud bath, zip-line, tubing, lunch
  • Getting there: 1.5-hour drive from JW Marriott. Most tour operators include transport.
  • Tip: The "adventure combo" packages are the best value — you get hot springs, mud bath, zip-line, tubing, and lunch for one price
  • Warning: April is very hot and dry in Guanacaste. Bring at least 2 liters of water per person for hiking.
