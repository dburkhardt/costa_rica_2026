Tamarindo Estuary Kayak

A peaceful 2-hour paddle through the mangrove channels of Las Baulas National Marine Park. This is the best local wildlife experience near Tamarindo — crocodiles, monkeys, iguanas, and dozens of bird species in a protected estuary just minutes from town.

The experience

What to expect

The Tamarindo estuary is part of Las Baulas National Marine Park, a protected area famous for leatherback sea turtle nesting (seasonal). The mangrove channels form a quiet labyrinth of green tunnels, tidal pools, and open waterways that are home to an impressive amount of wildlife — surprising for such a developed tourist area.

You'll paddle tandem kayaks (or single, your choice) through the mangroves with a naturalist guide who spots and identifies wildlife. American crocodiles are the star attraction — they're common and often seen basking on mudbanks or gliding silently through the water. You'll also see white-faced capuchins, howler monkeys, green iguanas, and a stunning array of birds: green and ringed kingfishers, boat-billed herons, tiger herons, mangrove warblers, and roseate spoonbills.

  • 2-hour guided kayak through mangrove channels
  • American crocodiles — commonly spotted from the kayak
  • White-faced capuchins and howler monkeys in the trees
  • Excellent birdwatching: kingfishers, herons, spoonbills, hawks
  • Green iguanas, Jesus Christ lizards (basilisks)
  • Calm, flat water — suitable for beginners and kids
  • Early morning tours see the most wildlife
  • Part of Las Baulas National Marine Park (protected area)

Practical info

How to book & what to bring

  • Booking: JW Marriott concierge, Native's Way Costa Rica, Tamarindo Mangrove Tours, or Blue Trailz
  • What to bring: Water shoes or sandals with straps, sunscreen, hat, water, camera (waterproof bag recommended), insect repellent
  • What's included: Kayak, paddle, life vest, naturalist guide
  • Getting there: 15-minute drive to the estuary launch point near Tamarindo
  • Tip: Book the earliest morning slot available — wildlife is most active at dawn and the estuary is glass-calm
  • Combo option: Some operators offer a kayak + snorkeling combo that fills a full morning
