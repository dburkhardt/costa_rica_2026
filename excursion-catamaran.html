Catamaran Sunset Cruise

Sail along the Guanacaste coast on a catamaran as the sun drops into the Pacific. Most cruises include a snorkeling stop, open bar, fresh fruit, and music. It's the quintessential Tamarindo afternoon — and April delivers some of the best sunset conditions of the year.

The experience

What to expect

You'll board a 40–60 foot catamaran at Tamarindo beach in the mid-afternoon. The first stop is usually a snorkeling spot along the rocky coastline where tropical fish, rays, and sea turtles are common in the clear April water.

After snorkeling, the boat sails south along the coast as the bar opens up. Fresh fruit, ceviche, and cocktails flow as the sun begins its descent. The final hour is pure magic — the sky turns pink and orange over the Pacific as you sail back to Tamarindo.

  • Departs mid-afternoon (~2:00 PM), returns after sunset (~6:30 PM)
  • Snorkeling stop with gear provided
  • Open bar — beer, cocktails, soft drinks
  • Fresh fruit, snacks, and often ceviche
  • Music and dancing on the trampoline nets
  • Dolphins frequently spotted along the coast
  • Suitable for all ages, including kids
  • Some operators offer smaller boats for a more intimate experience

Practical info

How to book & what to bring

  • Booking: JW Marriott concierge, or directly through Marlin del Rey, Blue Dolphin, or Seabird Sailing
  • What to bring: Swimsuit, towel, sunscreen (reef-safe), sunglasses, camera, light cover-up for evening
  • What's included: Snorkeling gear, open bar, fruit/snacks, on-board crew
  • Getting there: Most operators provide hotel pickup, or 15-min drive to Tamarindo beach
  • Tip: Book for your first full resort day — the sunset sets the tone for the whole trip
  • Seasickness: Catamarans are very stable, but take Dramamine 30 min before if you're prone
