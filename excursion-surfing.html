Surf Lesson at Playa Avellanas

One of Costa Rica's most consistent surf breaks is right next to the JW Marriott. Playa Avellanas offers gentle inside waves perfect for beginners and powerful outside sets for experienced surfers. April brings clean offshore conditions and warm water — no wetsuit needed.

The experience

What to expect

Playa Avellanas is a long, sandy beach backed by mangroves and dry tropical forest. The surf break is famous for "Little Hawaii" — a powerful reef break that draws experienced surfers from around the world. But the beach break closer to shore is perfect for lessons, with waist-high whitewater waves that are forgiving and fun.

Most lessons start with 15–20 minutes of beach instruction covering pop-up technique, board handling, and ocean safety. Then you're in the water for 1.5–2 hours with your instructor right beside you. First-timers typically stand up within the first 30 minutes.

  • Warm water (82°F / 28°C in April) — no wetsuit needed
  • Sandy bottom beach break — safe for beginners
  • Board and rash guard included in lesson price
  • Multiple surf schools operate on the beach
  • Great for kids age 8+ and adults of all fitness levels
  • Morning sessions have the cleanest conditions (before afternoon wind)
  • Lola's Beach Bar is right on the sand — post-surf ceviche and cold drinks

Practical info

How to book & what to bring

  • Booking: Ask the JW Marriott concierge, or book directly with Avellanas Surf School, Blue Trailz, or Iguana Surf
  • What to bring: Swimsuit, sunscreen (reef-safe), towel, water, change of clothes
  • What's included: Surfboard, rash guard, instructor, beach instruction + water time
  • Getting there: 5-minute drive from the JW Marriott. Parking available at the beach.
  • Tip: Book the first morning slot (7–8 AM) for the glassiest conditions and fewest crowds
  • After surfing: Lola's is an iconic beachfront restaurant right on Playa Avellanas — don't miss the fresh ceviche
