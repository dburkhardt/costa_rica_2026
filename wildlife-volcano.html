Why this option

Best for wildlife lovers

Three full days in Manuel Antonio means multiple chances to see sloths, capuchins, howler monkeys, and scarlet macaws. The Arenal night adds hot springs and a volcano experience without adding excessive driving.

Pros

  • 3 nights in Manuel Antonio — best chance to see sloths
  • Less total driving (~12 hrs vs ~18 hrs)

  • Arenal hot springs are a perfect wind-down

Cons

  • No luxury beach resort
  • Still one long drive day (MA → Arenal, 5–6 hrs)
  • Only 1 night at Arenal — not much margin

Day by day

The full plan

From arrival to departure, everything mapped out.

Day 0 · Thu Apr 16

Arrive San José

Air Transat TS 112 lands at SJO at 10:35 PM. Clear customs, pick up rental car, and drive 5 minutes to the Courtyard by Marriott SJO Alajuela. Crash for the night — early start tomorrow.

  • Flight: Air Transat TS 112, YYZ → SJO, departs 7:05 PM, arrives 10:35 PM
  • Travelers: Daniel, Emilie, Madison
  • Hotel: Courtyard by Marriott San José Airport Alajuela (5 min from SJO)
Day 1 · Fri Apr 17 · 3.5 hr drive

Drive to Manuel Antonio

Leave early morning for the 3.5-hour drive south along the Pacific coast to Manuel Antonio. Check into Si Como No or Tulemar and spend the afternoon exploring the area — maybe a sunset catamaran cruise or just settling into the jungle hotel.

  • Depart SJO area ~7:00 AM, arrive Manuel Antonio ~10:30 AM
  • Route: Highway 27 to Orotina, then coastal road south through Jacó and Quepos
  • Afternoon: Explore hotel grounds, swim, wildlife spotting from your balcony
  • Evening: Dinner at the hotel or in Quepos town
Day 2 · Sat Apr 18 · The sloth day

Manuel Antonio National Park

The main event. Gates open at 7 AM — hire a guide with a spotting scope for the best chance to see three-toed sloths, two-toed sloths, capuchins, howler monkeys, and scarlet macaws. After the guided walk, swim at Playa Manuel Antonio inside the park.

  • Arrive at park gates by 7:00 AM (before crowds)
  • Guided tour: 2–3 hours through primary rainforest trails
  • Sloth hotspots: look in cecropia trees along the Perezoso trail
  • Swimming at Playa Manuel Antonio and Playa Espadilla Sur
  • Park closes at 3:00 PM — no outside food, no plastic bags
  • Afternoon: relax at hotel pool, optional night wildlife tour
Day 3 · Sun Apr 19

Beaches, Wildlife & Adventure

A full free day in Manuel Antonio. Mix and match from the options below — or just lounge at the hotel pool with monkeys visiting your balcony.

  • Kayaking or stand-up paddleboarding along the coast
  • Sunset catamaran cruise with snorkeling
  • Whitewater rafting on the Savegre or Naranjo river
  • Explore Quepos town and the marina
  • More wildlife spotting — sloths are easier to find on a second day
Day 4 · Mon Apr 20 · 5–6 hr drive

Drive to Arenal & Hot Springs

The longest drive day. Leave Manuel Antonio early morning and head north through the mountains to La Fortuna and Arenal Volcano. Check in, then reward yourself with an evening at Tabacon Hot Springs — natural thermal pools set in lush jungle with views of the volcano.

  • Depart Manuel Antonio ~7:00 AM, arrive La Fortuna ~1:00 PM
  • Route: North through San Isidro, then via San Ramón to La Fortuna
  • Check into Arenal hotel, lunch, rest
  • Evening: Tabacon Thermal Resort — 20+ natural hot spring pools
  • Dinner at Tabacon (package available) or in La Fortuna town
Day 5 · Tue Apr 21 · 3–3.5 hr drive

Arenal Morning, Drive to SJO

Make the most of the morning at Arenal — hike to the La Fortuna Waterfall (500 steps down, 500 back up!) or walk the hanging bridges through the canopy. After lunch, drive 3 hours back to the airport area for your final night.

  • Morning option A: La Fortuna Waterfall — stunning 70-meter cascade
  • Morning option B: Arenal Hanging Bridges — suspension bridges through rainforest
  • Morning option C: Guided volcano hike along old lava flows
  • Depart La Fortuna ~1:00 PM, arrive SJO area ~4:00 PM
  • Hotel: Courtyard by Marriott SJO Alajuela
  • Early bedtime — 7:55 AM flight tomorrow!
Day 6 · Wed Apr 22

Fly Home

Early morning departure. Air Canada AC 956 departs SJO at 7:55 AM — be at the airport by 5:30 AM. Emilie and Madison fly home to Toronto; Daniel continues to Brazil.

  • Flight: Air Canada AC 956, SJO → YYZ, departs 7:55 AM, arrives 3:05 PM
  • Return travelers: Emilie & Madison
  • Daniel: separate flight to Brazil for work
Where you'll stay

The hotels

Two hotel options in Manuel Antonio, plus the Arenal area and airport transit hotel.

Manuel Antonio · Nights 1–3

Si Como No Resort, Spa & Wildlife Refuge

A sustainably-minded jungle resort perched above Manuel Antonio National Park. Two pools (one adults-only), two restaurants, a private 46-seat movie theater, butterfly atrium, and an on-site wildlife refuge. 5-minute shuttle to the beach.

Manuel Antonio · Nights 1–3 (Alternative)

Tulemar Resort

TripAdvisor's #1 hotel in Costa Rica. A gated 33-acre property with private bungalows and villas, a private beach, 4 pools, and incredible wildlife on the grounds — three types of monkeys, two types of sloths, scarlet macaws, and toucans right on the property.

Arenal · Night 4

Arenal / La Fortuna Area

The Arenal volcano region offers hot springs, waterfalls, hanging bridges, and one of the most dramatic landscapes in Costa Rica. Hotel TBD — options range from hot springs resorts like Tabacon to boutique lodges in La Fortuna.
