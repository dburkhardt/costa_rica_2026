Snorkeling at Catalinas Islands

A chain of rocky volcanic islands 20 minutes off the Guanacaste coast, the Catalinas are one of Costa Rica's top snorkeling and diving sites. April offers peak visibility — 60+ feet of crystal-clear water teeming with tropical fish, rays, and the chance to swim with giant manta rays.

The experience

What to expect

The Catalinas Islands (Islas Catalinas) are a cluster of about 20 small rocky islands roughly 3 miles off Playa Flamingo. The volcanic rock creates dramatic underwater topography — walls, arches, and swim-throughs that attract an incredible density of marine life.

The boat ride from Playa Flamingo takes about 20 minutes. You'll make 2–3 snorkeling stops at different sites around the islands. The water in April is warm (82°F), calm, and exceptionally clear. Schools of tropical fish swirl around the rocks, rays glide along the bottom, and sea turtles are regular visitors. The Catalinas are also one of the few places in Costa Rica where giant Pacific manta rays (wingspan up to 20 feet) are seen — most commonly from December through April.

  • 2–3 snorkeling stops at different island sites
  • Warm, calm water with 60+ feet visibility in April
  • Tropical fish: parrotfish, angelfish, pufferfish, triggerfish, sergeant majors
  • Rays: spotted eagle rays, cow-nose rays, and giant manta rays
  • Sea turtles: green and hawksbill turtles
  • White-tip reef sharks (harmless and shy)
  • Moray eels hiding in the rock crevices
  • All snorkeling gear provided; no experience necessary

Practical info

How to book & what to bring

  • Booking: JW Marriott concierge, or through Catalinas Diving, Resort Divers, or Tour Guanacaste
  • What to bring: Swimsuit, reef-safe sunscreen, towel, underwater camera/GoPro, anti-nausea meds if needed
  • What's included: Snorkeling gear (mask, snorkel, fins), boat ride, guide, fruit/drinks
  • Getting there: 30-min drive to Playa Flamingo, then 20-min boat ride. Some operators offer hotel pickup.
  • Tip: If you're a strong swimmer, ask about the deeper sites — that's where the mantas are most often spotted
  • Upgrade: Scuba diving available for certified divers — same sites but at greater depth
