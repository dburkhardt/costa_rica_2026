Animals

Costa Rica is home to nearly 5% of the world's biodiversity. Here are the creatures you're most likely to spot on the trip.

Wildlife spotting

What you'll see

From cheeky monkeys on the beach to sloths dozing in the canopy, Costa Rica delivers unforgettable wildlife encounters at every turn.

Primates

White-Faced Capuchin

The bold, clever monkeys of Manuel Antonio. They'll stare you down on the beach and steal your lunch if you're not careful. Found throughout the Central Pacific coast.

  • Best spot: Manuel Antonio National Park beaches
  • Often travel in groups of 15–20
Primates

Howler Monkey

You'll hear them before you see them — their roar carries for miles through the jungle canopy. Found in forests from Guanacaste to Manuel Antonio.

  • Best spot: Guanacaste coastal forests and Arenal hanging bridges
  • Loudest land animal — audible up to 3 miles away
Mammals

Two-Toed Sloth

Costa Rica's most beloved resident. They sleep up to 20 hours a day and move so slowly that algae grows on their fur, giving them a greenish tint.

  • Best spot: Arenal area and Manuel Antonio with a guide
  • A guide with a scope makes all the difference
More wildlife

Also keep an eye out for

Costa Rica's biodiversity goes far beyond the headline species.

Birds

Toucans & Macaws

  • Keel-billed toucans — rainbow beaks spotted near Arenal
  • Scarlet macaws — bright red, blue, and yellow, common near Manuel Antonio and Carara
  • Hummingbirds — dozens of species at feeders near Arenal lodges
Marine life

In the Pacific

  • Sailfish and mahi-mahi — targeted on fishing charters
  • Sea turtles — nesting season overlaps with the trip
  • Dolphins — frequently seen from fishing boats
  • Whales — humpbacks occasionally pass through in April
Reptiles & amphibians

Frogs, Lizards & More

  • Red-eyed tree frog — iconic, found on night walks near Arenal
  • Green iguana — sunbathing on branches everywhere
  • Crocodiles — Tárcoles River bridge is a famous viewing spot
  • Basilisk lizard — runs across water, earning its "Jesus Christ lizard" nickname
Insects

Tiny but Mighty

  • Blue morpho butterfly — iridescent wings flash through the jungle
  • Leaf-cutter ants — highways of green leaf fragments on the forest floor
  • Rhinoceros beetle — one of the strongest animals relative to body weight
