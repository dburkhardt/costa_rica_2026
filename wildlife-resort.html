Day by day

The full plan

From arrival to departure, everything mapped out.

Day 0 · Thu Apr 16

Arrive San José

Air Transat TS 112 lands at SJO at 10:35 PM. Clear customs, pick up rental car, and drive 5 minutes to the Courtyard by Marriott SJO Alajuela. Crash for the night — early start tomorrow.

  • Flight: Air Transat TS 112, YYZ → SJO, departs 7:05 PM, arrives 10:35 PM
  • Travelers: Daniel, Emilie, Madison
  • Hotel: Courtyard by Marriott San José Airport Alajuela (5 min from SJO)
Day 1 · Fri Apr 17 · 3.5 hr drive · The sloth day

Drive to Manuel Antonio & See Sloths

Leave SJO early for the 3.5-hour drive to Manuel Antonio. Head straight to the national park for an afternoon guided tour. This is your one shot at sloths — hire a guide with a spotting scope. The park is open until 3 PM, so aim to arrive by 11:30 AM latest.

  • Depart SJO area ~6:30 AM, arrive Manuel Antonio ~10:00 AM
  • Check bags at hotel, head straight to Manuel Antonio National Park
  • Guided tour: 2–3 hours — sloths, capuchins, howler monkeys, scarlet macaws
  • Swim at Playa Manuel Antonio inside the park
  • Park closes at 3:00 PM
  • Evening: check into hotel, dinner, early bedtime (long drive tomorrow)
Day 2 · Sat Apr 18 · 6–7 hr drive

Drive to JW Marriott Guanacaste

The longest drive of the trip. Head north from Manuel Antonio along the Pacific coast through Jacó and Puntarenas, then cut inland to the Interamericana highway and up through Cañas and Liberia to Guanacaste. No backtracking through San José — arrive in time for a sunset swim.

  • Depart Manuel Antonio ~7:00 AM
  • Route: Coastal road north through Quepos → Jacó → Puntarenas → Interamericana → Cañas → Liberia → Tamarindo
  • Arrive JW Marriott ~1:00–2:00 PM
  • Check in, explore the resort, hit the pool
  • Sunset on the beach — you've earned it after that drive
Day 3 · Sun Apr 19

Resort Day — Pool, Beach, Spa

No driving, no schedule. Wake up at the JW Marriott and choose your adventure — infinity pool, beach, spa, or all three. The resort sits on nearly 4,500 acres within the Hacienda Pinilla development with access to 5 different beaches.

  • Morning: beachfront infinity pool or Playa Mansita beach
  • Spa treatment at the full-service JW spa
  • Lunch at Azul Grill (poolside) or Tamarine (Asian fusion)
  • Afternoon: explore Hacienda Pinilla beaches
  • Sunset cocktails, dinner at Sabanero steakhouse
Day 4 · Mon Apr 20

Beach, Surf & Activities

Another full resort day. Try surfing at Playa Avellanas (one of Costa Rica's best breaks, right near the resort), horseback riding on the beach, or a round of golf at the 18-hole Hacienda Pinilla course.

  • Surf lesson at Playa Avellanas
  • Horseback riding along the beach
  • Golf at Hacienda Pinilla 18-hole course
  • Catamaran sunset cruise from Tamarindo
  • Day trip option: Rincón de la Vieja National Park (volcanic mud baths, waterfalls)
Day 5 · Tue Apr 21 · 4–5 hrs (or 7–8 via Arenal)

Drive to SJO (Optional Arenal Stop)

Last adventure day. Two options for getting back to San José:

  • Direct route (4–5 hrs): JW Marriott → Liberia → Interamericana → SJO. Leaves time for a morning beach session before departing.
  • Via Arenal (7–8 hrs): JW Marriott → Arenal/La Fortuna (4–5 hrs) → quick hot springs stop → SJO (3–3.5 hrs). Very long day but adds a volcano experience.
  • Either way, arrive SJO area by evening
  • Hotel: Courtyard by Marriott SJO Alajuela
  • Early bedtime — 7:55 AM flight tomorrow!
Day 6 · Wed Apr 22

Fly Home

Early morning departure. Air Canada AC 956 departs SJO at 7:55 AM — be at the airport by 5:30 AM. Emilie and Madison fly home to Toronto; Daniel continues to Brazil.

  • Flight: Air Canada AC 956, SJO → YYZ, departs 7:55 AM, arrives 3:05 PM
  • Return travelers: Emilie & Madison
  • Daniel: separate flight to Brazil for work
Where you'll stay

The hotels

One night at Manuel Antonio for sloths, three nights of luxury at the JW Marriott.

Manuel Antonio · Night 1

Si Como No or Tulemar

Just one night, so pick whichever has availability. Si Como No is a jungle eco-resort with two pools and a wildlife refuge. Tulemar is TripAdvisor's #1 hotel in Costa Rica with private bungalows and wildlife on the grounds.

Guanacaste · Nights 2–4

JW Marriott Guanacaste Resort & Spa

310 rooms on nearly 4,500 acres within the Hacienda Pinilla development. Beachfront infinity pool, 6 restaurants, full-service spa, 18-hole golf course, equestrian center, and access to 5 beaches. Titanium elite perks apply.

While you're there

Excursions & activities

Things to do during your 3 nights at the JW Marriott. Most can be booked through the resort concierge or local tour operators.

Surf Lesson at Playa Avellanas

One of Costa Rica's best surf breaks is right next to the resort. Lessons available for all levels — beginners welcome on the gentle inside break.

2–3 hours On-site

Catamaran Sunset Cruise

Sail from Tamarindo along the Guanacaste coast. Snorkeling stop, open bar, and a Pacific sunset from the water. Multiple operators run daily departures.

4 hours ~$85/person

Palo Verde Boat Safari

Boat tour down the Tempisque River through one of Central America's most important wetlands. Crocodiles, monkeys, iguanas, and hundreds of bird species.

Half day 1.5 hr drive

Horseback Riding on the Beach

Ride along the Hacienda Pinilla coastline at sunset. Available through the resort's equestrian center — no experience needed.

1.5 hours On-site

Golf at Hacienda Pinilla

18-hole championship course designed by Mike Young, right on the resort property. Ocean views, howler monkeys in the trees, and iguanas on the fairways.

4–5 hours On-site

Rincón de la Vieja Day Trip

Active volcano national park with volcanic mud baths, natural hot springs, and jungle waterfalls. A wilder alternative to Arenal, much closer to the resort.

Full day 1.5 hr drive

Tamarindo Estuary Kayak

Paddle through the mangroves of Las Baulas National Park. Crocodiles, monkeys, herons, kingfishers — the best local wildlife experience near Tamarindo.

2 hours ~$55/person

Face to Face Night Walk

Guided 2-hour night walk through protected dry forest near Tamarindo. Tarantulas, tree frogs, bats, giant moths, and stick insects — a different side of Costa Rica after dark.

2 hours Evening
