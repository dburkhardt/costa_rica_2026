Tropical Fruit

Costa Rica's markets and roadside stands overflow with fruit you can't get back home. Here's what to try — and where to find it.

Must-try fruit

The essentials

April is peak season for many tropical fruits. These are the ones worth seeking out at every feria (farmers' market), roadside stand, and restaurant.

Sweet & juicy

Mango

April is prime mango season in Costa Rica. You'll see bags of them at every roadside stand for almost nothing. The local varieties are smaller and more fragrant than what you find in North American supermarkets.

  • Peak season: March through May
  • Try them with lime and chili powder (mango con chile)
  • Look for the small, yellow "mango de agua" variety
Creamy & tropical

Cas (Guava)

Costa Rica's signature fruit for fresh juices. Cas is a tart, aromatic guava that's blended into "fresco de cas" — the unofficial national drink. Order it at any restaurant or soda.

  • Almost never eaten whole — always juiced or blended
  • Tart and refreshing, usually sweetened lightly
  • Available year-round at every soda and restaurant
Exotic

Guanabana (Soursop)

A spiky green fruit with creamy white flesh that tastes like a mix of strawberry and pineapple with a hint of coconut. Best in smoothies and ice cream.

  • Large and spiky on the outside, soft and creamy inside
  • Try guanabana ice cream or batido (smoothie)
  • Rich in vitamin C and antioxidants
Refreshing

Pipa (Green Coconut)

Roadside vendors hack the top off a green coconut with a machete and hand it to you with a straw. The water inside is cool, slightly sweet, and the best thing on a hot day.

  • Found at beaches and roadside stands everywhere
  • Ask the vendor to scoop out the soft jelly after drinking
  • Usually $1–2 per coconut
More to try

Keep tasting

Beyond the highlights, Costa Rica has a deep bench of tropical flavors.

Farm fresh

More Tropical Fruit

  • Rambutan (mamón chino) — hairy red shell, lychee-like flesh
  • Starfruit (carambola) — crisp, mildly sweet, beautiful star-shaped slices
  • Passion fruit (maracuyá) — intensely tart, incredible in juice
  • Plantain (plátano) — fried as patacones or sweet maduros with every meal
  • Papaya — served at breakfast with lime, pairs with granola
Where to find them

Best Spots

  • Roadside fruit stands — cheapest and freshest, especially on highways between towns
  • Ferias (farmers' markets) — Saturday morning markets in most towns
  • Sarapiquí fruit farms — The Explorer itinerary visits this area for tours
  • Tamarindo — The Resort itinerary includes fruit tasting here
  • Any restaurant — order a "fresco natural" (fresh fruit juice) with every meal
