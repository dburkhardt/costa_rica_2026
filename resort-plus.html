Day 0 · Thu Apr 16

Arrive & Settle

Land at SJO at 10:35 PM. Short shuttle to airport hotel.

  • Quick dinner at hotel or nearby

Day 1 · Fri Apr 17

Drive to Guanacaste

4-hour drive from SJO to the JW Marriott Guanacaste. Break up the drive with a stop at Tio Leo Coffee near Liberia.

  • Pick up rental car at SJO
  • 4-hour drive northwest via Pan-American Highway
  • Optional stop: Tio Leo Coffee farm (about 3 hrs in, near Liberia) — coffee roasting, cacao, empanada cooking class, tropical fruit garden. $45/person, 2–4 hrs. Incredible family experience.
  • Check in at JW Marriott Guanacaste Resort & Spa
  • Explore the resort — pools, beach, grounds
  • Golden-hour walk on Mansita Beach
  • Sunset dinner at the resort
Day 2 · Sat Apr 18

Resort Day or Excursion Day

Your first full day. Go lazy at the resort or pick from the excursion menu below.

  • Sleep in — resort breakfast buffet with fresh tropical fruit
  • Pool and beach day at the JW Marriott
  • Bike the Hacienda Pinilla trails
  • Walk to Lola's beachfront restaurant at low tide (about a 30-min beach walk)
  • Or pick an excursion from the menu below
Day 3 · Sun Apr 19

Resort Day or Excursion Day

Second full day at the resort. Mix and match from the excursion menu.

  • Another morning at the resort, or head out for adventure
  • Tamarindo is 20 minutes away for shopping, surfing, and restaurants
  • Playa Conchal (crushed seashell beach, great snorkeling) is 35 min north
  • Or pick an excursion from the menu below
Day 4 · Mon Apr 20

Scenic Drive to Arenal

Checkout from the JW Marriott and head east to La Fortuna. The drive is beautiful — plan stops along the way.

  • Morning checkout from JW Marriott
  • Stop 1 (about 1.5 hrs): Catarata Llanos de Cortés — stunning waterfall right off the highway, $2 parking, 5-min walk down, safe for swimming
  • Stop 2 (about 1.5 hrs): Roadside fruit stands near Cañas — mango, papaya, pineapple, pipa (green coconut water). Lunch at Restaurante Mimi (cheap, local, authentic)
  • Stop 3 (about 2.5 hrs): Café y Macadamia on Lake Arenal — coffee, pastries, lake views. If crowded, try Café con Amor in Nuevo Arenal
  • Stop 4 (about 3 hrs): Lake Arenal Mirador viewpoint — pull-off with picnic benches, volcano and lake views, free
  • Arrive La Fortuna, check in to Arenal lodge
  • Evening soak at Tabacón or Baldi volcanic hot springs
Day 5 · Tue Apr 21

Arenal Morning & Return to SJO

Morning adventure at Arenal, then drive back to the San Jose area for the final night.

  • Morning: Mistico Arenal Hanging Bridges — canopy walk through the rainforest, spot sloths and toucans
  • Or: La Fortuna Waterfall hike (500 steps down and back up — worth it)
  • Late morning drive to San José area (about 2.5 hours)
  • Stop at Frutera La Amistad in Coyolar for last-chance tropical fruit
  • Souvenir and coffee shopping along the way
  • Check in to airport hotel
  • Early night — Emilie & Madison fly at 7:55 AM
Days 2 and 3

Excursion menu

Pick one or two from the list below for your resort days. All depart from or near the JW Marriott. Book ahead for fishing charters and the catamaran.

Water

Snorkeling at Catalinas Islands

Half-day morning trip from Flamingo. Boat ride to volcanic rock formations teeming with marine life — reef sharks, rays, tropical fish, sea turtles. Two guided snorkel sessions with snacks and drinks on board.

  • About $85–140 per person
  • Half day (7 hrs including transport)
  • 35 min drive plus 40 min boat
Water

Sport Fishing Charter

Half-day charter out of Flamingo or Tamarindo. April is prime season — sailfish, mahi-mahi, roosterfish, tuna. Boat fits all 3 of you comfortably. Some captains will clean your catch for dinner.

  • About $500–800 per boat (half day, smaller boats)
  • Half day
  • 30 min drive to marina
Water

Sunset Catamaran Cruise

Afternoon sail along the Pacific coast with snorkeling stop, open bar, fresh food, paddleboards and kayaks. Watch the sunset from the water.

  • About $85–95 per person
  • 4–5 hrs
  • 20–35 min drive
Wildlife

Palo Verde River Safari

Boat cruise through one of Costa Rica's most biodiverse wetlands. Crocodiles, howler monkeys, iguanas, and dozens of exotic bird species. Quiet, peaceful, fascinating.

  • About $59 per person
  • Half day
  • 1.5 hr drive
Wildlife

Bijagua Sloth Search

Drive into the rainforest lowlands between Miravalles and Tenorio volcanoes. Guided walk through primary rainforest — two-toed sloths, red-eyed tree frogs, poison dart frogs, toucans.

  • About $29+ per person
  • Half day
  • 2–2.5 hr drive
Wildlife

Diamante Eco Sanctuary

World-class animal sanctuary near Guanacaste. Sloths, monkeys, toucans, pumas, and a stunning blue butterfly garden. Educational and up-close. Great for a 12-year-old.

  • About $39 per person
  • Half day
  • 1 hr drive
Wildlife

Night Wildlife Walk

Guided 2-hour walk through dry forest near Tamarindo after dark. Tarantulas, tree frogs, snakes, sleeping butterflies, kinkajous. Headlamps provided. A completely different world after sunset.

  • About $30–40 per person
  • 2 hrs starting at 6 PM
  • 20 min drive
Adventure

Guachipelin Volcano Combo

Full-day adventure at Rincón de la Vieja volcano: zip-line canopy tour, horseback riding, river tubing (Class III), volcanic hot springs and mud bath. Lunch included.

  • About $135 per person
  • Full day
  • 1.5–2 hr drive
Adventure

Surfing Lessons in Tamarindo

Tamarindo is one of Costa Rica's best beginner surf spots. Warm water, consistent waves, tons of schools. Perfect for a family morning.

  • About $50–75 per person
  • 2 hrs
  • 20 min drive
Chill

Beach Hopping

Drive up the coast hitting hidden beaches: Playa Conchal (crushed seashells, clear snorkeling), Playa Mina, Playa San Juanillo. Roadside fruit stands and howler monkeys along the way. No booking needed.

  • Free (just gas)
  • Self-guided
  • Various distances
Chill

Tamarindo Town Day

Browse boutique shops, try surf-town restaurants, fresh smoothie stands, and the weekly farmers market (Saturday mornings). Pair with a surf lesson or just wander.

  • Free to cheap
  • Self-paced
  • 20 min drive
Chill

Turtle Nesting Tour

Evening trip to a protected beach north of Tamarindo. Watch Olive Ridley or green sea turtles dig nests and lay eggs. Guided, red-light only. April is tail end of peak season — possible but not guaranteed.

  • About $75 per person
  • Evening departing around 5:30 PM
  • 40 min drive plus walk
