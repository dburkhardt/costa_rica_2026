Face to Face Night Walk

After dark, Costa Rica's dry forest comes alive with a completely different cast of characters. This guided 2-hour night walk through a protected forest near Tamarindo reveals tarantulas, tree frogs, bats, giant moths, and dozens of creatures you'd never see during the day.

The experience

What to expect

The Face to Face Night Walk takes you into one of the last remaining patches of protected tropical dry forest in the Tamarindo area. Starting at dusk, your guide leads a small group (max 8 people) along forest trails with flashlights and UV lights, stopping to examine every creature encountered along the way.

The dry forest ecosystem is surprisingly rich at night. Your guide will find tarantulas in their burrows, tailless whip scorpions under bark, sleeping birds on branches, and a remarkable variety of frogs — yellow tree frogs, milk frogs, and if you're lucky, the iconic red-eyed tree frog. Giant moths with 6-inch wingspans flutter to UV light traps. Bats swoop overhead hunting insects. It's a completely different world from the daytime beach experience.

  • 2-hour guided walk starting at dusk (~5:30 PM)
  • Small groups (max 8 people) for intimate experience
  • Tarantulas, wolf spiders, tailless whip scorpions
  • Tree frogs: yellow tree frog, milk frog, red-eyed tree frog
  • Giant moths, stick insects, leaf-cutter ant highways
  • Bats, geckos, sleeping birds, boa constrictors
  • UV lights reveal fluorescent scorpions and fungi
  • Expert naturalist guide with extensive local knowledge
  • Perfect for kids who love bugs and creepy-crawlies

Practical info

How to book & what to bring

  • Booking: facetofacenightwalk.com, Airbnb Experiences, or JW Marriott concierge
  • What to bring: Closed-toe shoes, long pants, insect repellent, camera with flash, light jacket
  • What's included: Flashlight/headlamp, UV light, naturalist guide, water
  • Getting there: 15-minute drive from JW Marriott. Some operators offer pickup.
  • Tip: Wear long pants and closed-toe shoes — you're walking through forest at night. Sandals are a bad idea.
  • For kids: This is a huge hit with kids (especially ages 6–14) who love bugs and are okay with spiders. Younger kids may be spooked by the dark.
  • Timing: Book for one of your resort evenings — it starts at dusk and you're back by 8 PM, in time for a late dinner.
